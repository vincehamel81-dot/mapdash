MapDashRun - Notes

Version 1.2.6 (2026-07-14)

  • Wind direction never repeats twice in a row - fixes long streaks (e.g. 5x West) pushing most clouds permanently outside the play area
  • If a majority of clouds have already drifted outside the bbox, the next wind pick is biased back toward blowing them in instead of picking fully at random

Version 1.2.5 (2026-07-14)

  • Fixed the real cause of clouds freezing/stuttering: the movement tick was reading a stale, only-every-20-seconds-refreshed snapshot of room data instead of the current one, so most ticks drifted from the same frozen starting point instead of compounding - clouds should now move continuously every tick with no freeze window

Version 1.2.4 (2026-07-13)

  • Fixed a race condition where two independent cloud-update timers in Survival could clobber each other's writes, keeping the count stuck around 200-220 instead of growing toward 500 - merged into a single writer
  • Throttled cloud rendering to ~12fps instead of full 60fps - full-rate updates on a few-hundred-polygon layer were heavy enough to stutter unrelated map interaction
  • Survival wind capped at 1000 km/h (was uncapped, up to ~1400)

Version 1.2.3 (2026-07-13)

  • Clouds no longer teleport between ticks - positions now smoothly interpolate on every frame client-side, independent of the (still infrequent, Supabase-friendly) authoritative movement tick

Version 1.2.2 (2026-07-13)

  • Fixed a Supabase realtime rate-limit crash: NPCs and Finder items each broadcast their own position individually every 200ms - with Survival's 99-bot rooms that was up to ~500 sends/second, enough to 429 the whole project (including unrelated concurrent games). Now batched into one broadcast per tick regardless of how many NPCs/items there are.
  • Fixed Survival clouds barely accumulating: the 10x wind covers the out-of-bounds prune margin in as little as 9-30 seconds, so clouds were dying almost as fast as they spawned. Survival now relies on cloud lifetime (7-15min) instead of bbox-exit pruning.
  • Added a live cloud count next to the survivor count in Survival
  • Survival leaderboard now shows a "Perfect scores" divider when more players are untouched (1000 HP) than fit in the top 5

Version 1.2.1 (2026-07-13)

  • Fixed a site-breaking crash ("Cannot access before initialization") introduced in v1.2 - a new effect referenced updateRoom before its declaration in the file

Version 1.2 (2026-07-13)

  • Survival overhauled into a fixed 100-player mode: host starts alone or with friends, the rest auto-fills with NPCs at start
  • Survival roster now shows top 5 + your own rank (highlighted), plus a survivor count, instead of every player
  • Survival: escalating deadlines - eliminated if under 50 HP at 2 min, under 100 at 5 min, under 200 at 8 min
  • Survival clouds move 3x more often, wind is 10x, and a new cloud is forced in every 2 seconds (200 to ~500 over a round)
  • "Add cloud" and "Add NPC" are disabled in Survival - both are now automatic

Version 1.1.1 (2026-07-13)

  • Survival clouds doubled again, 100 to 200 - with 50-player rooms, too many bots were still surviving a full round untouched

Version 1.1 (2026-07-13)

  • Added Esri NatGeo as a 4th map theme
  • Survival: NPCs now take cloud damage and regenerate the same way real players do, instead of driving through clouds unaffected
  • Room capacity raised to 50 players (Single stays solo)
  • Survival clouds are now exactly 2x general size (up from 1.75x)

Version 1.0 (2026-07-13)

  • Start of v1: 7 modes - Single, Team, Survival, Finder (Easy), Finder (Roaming), Finder (Hard), Tag
  • Finder (Roaming) - items move around with distinct personalities (11 collectibles, 10 spawn per round at random)
  • 59 avatars, including a first batch of fixed-color and animated ones
  • Single/Team now play across a much wider area (the full synced street network) instead of the tighter Survival/Tag/Finder box
  • Survival clouds reworked: spread across the whole map, much denser, longer-lived, no more instant-kill spawns
  • Off-screen radar indicators for Finder items and Tag
  • Room chat with friend requests, auto-scroll
  • Rooms hold up to 30 players (Single stays solo)
