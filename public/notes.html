MapDashRun - Notes

Version 1.10.11 (2026-07-17)

  • Debug markers (missing connections / risky intersections) now show up in Single mode too, not just the tight-bbox modes - same reviewable ~300-item list either way, since it's always computed from the same Québec-only scope regardless of which map is actually being driven on

Version 1.10.10 (2026-07-17)

  • Fixed the previous turn-signal fix having a blind spot: when a street forks into two branches under its own name (e.g. a service road splitting into two, both still called the same thing), left and right could both send you down the same one, since the fix always favored whichever option had a different name - even when the real correct turn was the same-named branch. Root-caused live from a reported spot ("Bretelle Boul. Père-Lelièvre, Sortie 8" forking into itself plus a bus access road) where both directions picked the bus road. Now a barely-off-straight same-named option still gets correctly ignored (the original highway-ramp case), but a real same-named fork gets treated as a real fork

Version 1.10.9 (2026-07-17)

  • Scaled the debug markers back down to what's actually reviewable: they now only show up in tight-bbox modes (Survival, Tag, Finder, etc.) where the count matches the ~300 real list, instead of Single/Team's much bigger map (Québec + Lévis) which was showing thousands. Also dropped the shallow-fork markers from the live overlay entirely - the turn-signal fix already covers all of them generically, so they were never meant for one-by-one checking. Bumped marker size/contrast for readability

Version 1.10.8 (2026-07-17)

  • Fixed severe lag introduced by the previous update's debug markers - driving with "Debug: street graph" on was nearly unplayable. Root cause: each numbered marker was re-positioning itself on every single frame of driving (thousands of them, ~4700 on the full map), not just when actually needed. The colored dots now render as one cheap map layer instead, and the numbered labels only exist for whatever's currently on-screen (refreshed a few times a second, not every frame) - same information, no lag

Version 1.10.7 (2026-07-17)

  • Debug overlay now shows numbered markers for every flagged spot (missing connections in orange, risky intersections in red, shallow forks in purple), so they can be checked one at a time and reported by number instead of by description. Hover a marker for details. Turn on "Debug: street graph" to see them

Version 1.10.6 (2026-07-17)

  • Fixed flashing a turn signal doing nothing at shallow highway forks (e.g. an exit ramp splitting off the mainline at just a few degrees). Root-caused live from an actual console repro: neither the ramp nor the mainline was clearly enough "left" or "right" of straight ahead to satisfy the turn-signal logic, so it quietly ignored the signal and just kept going straight - which mostly happened to be the mainline. Now, when nothing is clearly enough to one side, an active turn signal at least gets you off the street you're currently on instead of defaulting to "closest to straight"

Version 1.10.5 (2026-07-17)

  • Re-added real intersection splitting where two streets' lines actually cross in space (highway ramps, X-intersections) - pure geometry, no guessing involved, so it can't invent a fake connection. Tested two other "should be safe" reconnection ideas (matching a dead-end to a nearby street's interior point; stitching a divided road's two carriageways back together) and both turned out to make things worse once measured properly, not better - so they're staying off for now rather than shipping something that just trades one kind of bad intersection for another. Net effect versus 1.10.4: about the same clean baseline (~316 flagged gaps, 89 confusing intersections), plus real X-crossings now connect. Still driving with the same official street data - see "Debug: street graph" to inspect it directly, and keep reporting spots that feel wrong so they can be fixed one at a time instead of with a blanket rule

Version 1.10.4 (2026-07-16)

  • Experimental: the street map now trusts the official Ville de Québec road data as-is instead of guessing which nearby streets should connect. That guessing (originally meant to close small gaps) was also inventing connections that don't exist in reality - like the fake "9-way" tangle inside highway interchanges, or streets like Côte d'Abraham appearing wrongly linked to Rue Olivier-Robitaille. Turning it off drops false/confusing intersections from ~579 to 89 citywide, at the cost of some real gaps reappearing (58 to ~300) until they're fixed properly, one pattern at a time. Turn on "Debug: street graph" while driving to see it directly and report what still looks wrong

Version 1.10.3 (2026-07-16)

  • Fixed a crash that broke the entire site (blank page on load): widening the dangling-street snap tolerance in the last update exposed a pre-existing edge case that only showed up against the full map including Lévis - a street could end up referencing a piece of road that had already been replaced elsewhere, crashing before the map ever rendered. Verified fixed against the exact same data that crashed

Version 1.10.2 (2026-07-16)

  • Widened how far a dead-end street will look for another nearby street to connect to (25m to 65m, tested directly against the real map data - real gaps go up to 60m, and going wider doesn't cause any new wrong turns elsewhere). Fixes 3 out of every 4 remaining "street stops short of where it should obviously continue" spots citywide (244 down to 60)

Version 1.10.1 (2026-07-16)

  • Further navigation cleanup: the divided-road fix from the last update left small "self-loop" fragments behind at ~2,700 spots citywide (a street segment that starts and ends at the same intersection - pure merge leftover, not a real road). These are now removed too. Wrong-turn intersections dropped again, 696 to 572, and short-block chains nearly halved, 6,774 to 3,248

Version 1.10.0 (2026-07-16)

  • Fixed a real, widespread navigation bug: divided roads (boulevards and highways with a median) were often represented as two separate near-parallel lines - one per direction of travel - reconnected by a short "bridge" a bit further along. Confirmed directly (Boulevard Masson/Rue Morand near Rue Gide): this is exactly what made turning right feel like it "didn't register", and what showed up as stray disconnected-looking line segments on the map. Both carriageways now collapse into one clean line, and true duplicate street segments (two different shapes between the same two points) are removed too
  • Concretely: wrong-turn intersections dropped from 802 to 696 citywide (324 still involve a highway/ramp), and short-block chains (which strain turn-signal timing) dropped from 9,661 to 6,774

Version 1.9.3 (2026-07-16)

  • Spectator count pill now sits beside the speed readout instead of above it - no longer pushes speed up or covers it when the name list pops open

Version 1.9.2 (2026-07-16)

  • Players now see a 👁 count next to their speed when someone's spectating their game - click it to see who. No spectator, no pill; leaving spectate mode removes you from the count right away

Version 1.9.1 (2026-07-16)

  • Fixed a real bug in Spectate mode: your own name/icon marker was never actually hidden while spectating, so you'd see yourself sitting at your last driving position, and pressing arrow keys would show turn-signal arrows attached to nothing. The whole marker is now hidden while spectating - real players correctly never saw it in the first place, since spectating never broadcasts a position

Version 1.9.0 (2026-07-16)

  • New: Spectate. From Chat, a friend (or anyone Online) who's mid-round now shows a "Spectate" button instead of "Join" - watch their game with a free pan/zoom camera and a read-only sidebar (players remaining, health/items found, who's It, etc.), no seat taken in their room. An "✕" button top-right returns to the lobby. Non-round modes like Team still show "Join" as before

Version 1.8.4 (2026-07-16)

  • Cloud mode: found the actual cause of "still thousands of dots" even in an otherwise-dense view - any lightly-overlapping point was crossing the color ramp's low threshold and rendering as its own tiny visible blob instead of fading out. Raised that threshold a lot higher (only genuinely-fused clusters render at all now), pulled the satellite puffs in tighter against the core, and roughly doubled point density again

Version 1.8.3 (2026-07-16)

  • Fixed a real bug behind "looks great zoomed out, hundreds of little dots zoomed in": the cloud's blur radius is measured in screen pixels, but its points are spread out in fixed real-world distances - zooming in makes those points drift much farther apart on screen than the blur radius was scaling to match. Radius now roughly doubles every zoom level instead of barely increasing, so the same dense look now holds at any zoom, not just zoomed out

Version 1.8.2 (2026-07-16)

  • Cloud mode's cloud is noticeably denser at every size - point count now scales with the chosen Size (was a fixed count regardless of size, so XL/XXL actually looked sparser, not bigger), satellite puffs pulled in close to the core instead of floating off to the side as separate mini-clouds, and each point is biased much closer to its own cluster's center instead of spreading evenly out to the edge - the earlier version still read as scattered dots in a live screenshot, this should read as one dense mass with a soft fringe

Version 1.8.1 (2026-07-16)

  • Cloud mode is now solo (like Single) instead of a multiplayer room - no room code to create or join, just pick Cloud and drive. Simpler and avoids the room-sync machinery entirely for a mode that was never meant to be competitive
  • Cloud mode now shares Single/Team's full wide play area (Lévis and the other outer towns included), not the tighter Survival/Tag/Finder box
  • Added two bigger Size options, XL and XXL, for clouds that dwarf the map
  • Fixed a real bug: picking a Direction and then pressing a driving key (especially "S", also a direction option) could silently change the Direction back - the dropdown kept keyboard focus after a click, so the very next key press was captured by the dropdown instead of the car
  • Cloud shape rebalanced: one clearly dominant core with a couple of smaller puffs around it, instead of several similar-sized scattered blobs that read as "lots of small independent clouds"

Version 1.8.0 (2026-07-15)

  • New 8th mode: Cloud - playful, non-competitive, same instant-play/no-timer/no-health/no-score shape as Team. One giant, soft, realistic-looking cloud drifts across the map in a fixed direction and respawns on the opposite side once it's fully gone - shape validated against a real weather-map reference photo before building (a wispy, uneven point-scatter texture, not a solid outline)
  • Cloud mode: host controls for Direction, Speed, and Size in the sidebar, adjustable anytime (no separate lobby/start step, matching Team)

Version 1.7.0 (2026-07-15)

  • Fixed a real bug: HP and cloud count shown in the roster were leftover from your *previous* round, not reset until a new round actually started - a brand new "waiting" room could show 418 HP and 213 clouds active before anyone had even pressed Start
  • Tag: Turbo now works for both It and regular players (previously silently disabled in Tag on purpose, but that read as broken rather than intentional - now stacks with It's speed bonus)
  • Tag: host can now pick who starts as It before starting the round, instead of it always being random
  • Finder (Hard) now spawns 5 items instead of 10 - finding 10 with no map assistance was very hard/long
  • Chat: fixed a real layout bug where a message with no spaces (e.g. one long repeated character) could push the online/friends sidebar off-screen instead of wrapping
  • Start point now also syncs via Supabase (not just localStorage), so it follows you across devices instead of resetting every time you switch

Version 1.6.0 (2026-07-15)

  • Mobile support, phase 1: the sidebar (roster/chat/settings) is now a slide-out drawer below a phone-width breakpoint instead of a fixed 320px column blocking the map
  • Mobile support, phase 2: on-screen D-pad and Turbo button for driving on touch devices - no keyboard required

Version 1.5.0 (2026-07-14)

  • Start point now persists across sessions (localStorage) instead of resetting to default every game
  • Scores now start at 1000 (not 0) and can never drop below 0, matching the intended design
  • Scores table now supports multiple game types (game_type column) ahead of Finder/Tag getting their own scoring later
  • Fixed a widespread street-graph issue: two directly-reported "broken street" spots turned out to be part of a much larger pattern (1,244 similar gaps found city-wide) where the graph's node-merge tolerance was just barely too tight (12-15m) for real gaps in the source data (up to ~25m) - widened it after directly testing that doing so only improved graph quality, no downside found

Version 1.4.0 (2026-07-14)

  • Survival scores now actually persist across games (an atomic Postgres increment, not a naive read-then-write) - a running -100..+100 total per player
  • New 🏆 Leaderboard button next to the mode buttons, showing every player's all-time score ordered highest first
  • Added auto-cleanup for abandoned rooms (host tab closed mid-round, nothing left to mark it finished) - a scheduled database job deletes any room untouched for 30 minutes, regardless of status
  • Added Wendake and L'Ancienne-Lorette streets - two small municipalities entirely surrounded by Québec that were previously visible-but-roadless holes on the map, now drivable in every mode

Version 1.3.0 (2026-07-14)

  • Fixed a real bug: NPC and cloud simulation had no round-status check at all, so both kept running indefinitely after a round finished - HP kept changing and clouds kept growing even after "X won!" was already showing. Also fixed the round-finish timer display, which recomputed against the current time forever instead of freezing at the actual finish moment.
  • Room code input now clears when you're eliminated (not just when you quit), fixing a "room already exists" collision when trying to create a new one while still watching your old one finish
  • Survival roster now ranks eliminated players by elimination order (first out finishes last) instead of by their frozen health value
  • Survival scoring: every player is ranked -100 to +100 based on final placement (1st = +100, last = -100), shown on the round-finish report. Ties share the better score/rank rather than splitting it. Not yet persisted across games - a cross-session leaderboard would need its own database table, planned as a follow-up.
  • Survival: nearby players/bots now show their live HP next to their name on the map, not just in the roster
  • Survival clouds now spawn mostly near the bbox edges instead of uniformly - players were consistently surviving better near the borders because the center saw disproportionately more cloud coverage

Version 1.2.6 (2026-07-14)

  • Wind direction never repeats twice in a row - fixes long streaks (e.g. 5x West) pushing most clouds permanently outside the play area
  • If a majority of clouds have already drifted outside the bbox, the next wind pick is biased back toward blowing them in instead of picking fully at random

Version 1.2.5 (2026-07-14)

  • Fixed the real cause of clouds freezing/stuttering: the movement tick was reading a stale, only-every-20-seconds-refreshed snapshot of room data instead of the current one, so most ticks drifted from the same frozen starting point instead of compounding - clouds should now move continuously every tick with no freeze window

Version 1.2.4 (2026-07-13)

  • Fixed a race condition where two independent cloud-update timers in Survival could clobber each other's writes, keeping the count stuck around 200-220 instead of growing toward 500 - merged into a single writer
  • Throttled cloud rendering to ~12fps instead of full 60fps - full-rate updates on a few-hundred-polygon layer were heavy enough to stutter unrelated map interaction
  • Survival wind capped at 1000 km/h (was uncapped, up to ~1400)

Version 1.2.3 (2026-07-13)

  • Clouds no longer teleport between ticks - positions now smoothly interpolate on every frame client-side, independent of the (still infrequent, Supabase-friendly) authoritative movement tick

Version 1.2.2 (2026-07-13)

  • Fixed a Supabase realtime rate-limit crash: NPCs and Finder items each broadcast their own position individually every 200ms - with Survival's 99-bot rooms that was up to ~500 sends/second, enough to 429 the whole project (including unrelated concurrent games). Now batched into one broadcast per tick regardless of how many NPCs/items there are.
  • Fixed Survival clouds barely accumulating: the 10x wind covers the out-of-bounds prune margin in as little as 9-30 seconds, so clouds were dying almost as fast as they spawned. Survival now relies on cloud lifetime (7-15min) instead of bbox-exit pruning.
  • Added a live cloud count next to the survivor count in Survival
  • Survival leaderboard now shows a "Perfect scores" divider when more players are untouched (1000 HP) than fit in the top 5

Version 1.2.1 (2026-07-13)

  • Fixed a site-breaking crash ("Cannot access before initialization") introduced in v1.2 - a new effect referenced updateRoom before its declaration in the file

Version 1.2 (2026-07-13)

  • Survival overhauled into a fixed 100-player mode: host starts alone or with friends, the rest auto-fills with NPCs at start
  • Survival roster now shows top 5 + your own rank (highlighted), plus a survivor count, instead of every player
  • Survival: escalating deadlines - eliminated if under 50 HP at 2 min, under 100 at 5 min, under 200 at 8 min
  • Survival clouds move 3x more often, wind is 10x, and a new cloud is forced in every 2 seconds (200 to ~500 over a round)
  • "Add cloud" and "Add NPC" are disabled in Survival - both are now automatic
